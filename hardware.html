Hardware

SatNOGS includes a hardware stack, detailing the construction and deployment of a complete ground station for satellites. Materials and resources used as readily available, affordable and to the extend possible re-using existing projects.

Tripod
Portable, easy to assemble

Designed and build with mobility in mind, this lightweight tripod is made of 3D-printed parts alluminium profiles and bolts to keep everything together.

Tracking mechanism
Printable, parametric

Our tracking mechanism is designed from scratch, parametric by default, 3D printed in under 8 hours. Utilizing two NEMA14 stepper motors we have uncostrained movement in Altitude and Azimuth.

Driving electronics
Open-source, accurate

Two Stepper motor drivers and an Arduino controlling them, are our driving electronics. Simple yet accurate.

Antennas
Custom, modular

Lightweight antennas designed to meet satellite communication specifications. SatNOGS includes designs and how-tos for 70cm and 2m band Yagi antennas. Parabolic antenna design is in the works too.

Single-board computer
Integrated, low power

Our primary configuration includes the BeagleBone Black or the TP-Link TL-WR703N as a single-board computer. Running Debian or OpenWrt, exposes all ground station functionality (status, tracking, signal receiving and processing) over LAN or WiFi for extreme mobility or remote operation.

Receiver
Versatile, affordable

Based on the versatile RTL2832U chip , SatNOGS is using R820T RTL-SDR as the default signal receiver and tuner.
